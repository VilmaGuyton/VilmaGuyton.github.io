2. Starting the Recording#

Recording Android screen with PC is a great way to capture footage of your gaming sessions, create how-to videos, or just store a memory. With the right tools, recording your Android device’s screen on your computer is simple and straightforward. Here are the steps to get started:

Firstly, install the Android SDK on your computer. This is a suite of tools used for developing Android apps, and it contains the Android Debug Bridge or ADB, which is necessary for screen recording.

Secondly, turn on USB debugging on your Android device. To do this, enable the Developer options in your device’s settings and then look for USB debugging. Then plug in your Android device to your computer using a USB cable.

Thirdly, launch the ADB on your computer. Simply type in ‘adb devices’ in the command prompt or Terminal window. The ADB should start and you should be able to see your device in the list.

Finally, use the ADB to record your device’s screen. All you have to do is type in ‘adb shell screenrecord’ and the recording will begin. When you’re done, press ‘Ctrl + C’ and the recording will stop. You can find your video in your device’s internal storage.
