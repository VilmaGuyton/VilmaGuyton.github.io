Q1. What do I need to record my Android screen with my PC? A1. You need a USB cable, an Android device capable of running Android 4.4 or higher, and a computer running Windows 7 or higher. Additionally, you’ll need to download the ADB (Android Debug Bridge) tools onto your computer. This can be done from the official Android developers website.

Q2. How do I connect my Android device to my PC? A2. First, make sure your device is in developer mode and USB debugging is turned on. Then, connect your device to your PC using a USB cable. Your PC should recognize the device.

Q3. How do I install the ADB tools on my computer? A3. The ADB tools can be downloaded from the official Android developers website. Follow the instructions for installation, and make sure the ADB tools are added to your system path.

Q4. How do I start recording my Android device’s screen on my PC? A4. First, make sure that your device is connected to your PC and that ADB tools are installed. Then, open a command prompt in the ADB directory and enter the command “adb shell screenrecord [file name]”. This will start the recording process.

Q5. How do I stop recording? A5. To stop recording, press CTRL + C on your keyboard. Your recording will be saved in the ADB folder on your computer.
