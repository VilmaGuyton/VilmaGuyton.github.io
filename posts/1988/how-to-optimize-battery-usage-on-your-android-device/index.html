A. Identifying Battery-Draining Apps#

Optimizing the battery on your Android device can be a hassle, but it’s a great way to make sure the device is running at its best. Here are a few steps to optimize battery usage on your Android device:

First, turn off Bluetooth, GPS, NFC and Wi-Fi when not in use as these features can drain your battery quickly. To do this, open the settings menu, scroll down and tap the Network & Internet option.

Next, disable all auto-updates for apps in the background. To do this, open the Google Play Store, tap the three-line icon and select Settings. Under the App Update tab, choose one of the three options: Do not auto-update apps, auto-update over Wi-Fi only, or auto-update at any time. This will lessen the amount of battery used when apps are updated in the background.

Finally, reduce the amount of brightness on your screen by enabling the ‘Adaptive Brightness’ feature. This feature allows the device to adjust the brightness according to lighting conditions, reducing the amount of energy used. To turn this on, open your device’s settings, go to the Display or Display and Brightness option and enable the Adaptive Brightness feature.
