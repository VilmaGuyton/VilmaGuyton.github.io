Do you want to extend your Android phone’s battery life? Check out these 5 easy tips to check app battery usage on Android to help you get the most out of your device. Do you ever feel like your Android device runs out of battery way too quickly? Are you curious about which apps are consuming the most of your device’s power? If you answered yes to both these questions, you have come to the right place!

Here, we will teach you 5 easy tips to check app battery usage on Android. By following these steps, you can keep track of your device’s battery consumption and find out which apps are draining the most of it. Additionally, you can take the necessary steps to modify your apps or settings to optimise battery life.

The first step is to open your device’s settings. Once you are in the settings, navigate to the battery option and click on it. Here, you can see the battery usage of all the apps on your device. This list will show you how much battery each app is consuming and the percentage of your battery life it is taking up.

The second step is to look for any unusually-high levels of battery consumption. If you see any app using an unusually-high amount of battery, you can investigate further. Click on the app to learn more about its battery usage.

The third step is to find out exactly how that app is consuming your device’s battery. Here, you can see all the activities that the app is carrying out and contributing to battery consumption. This will help you identify the culprit activities and take the necessary steps to reduce them.

The fourth step is to modify the apps’ settings. You can opt out of any activities that are causing a lot of battery consumption, turn off background services or make other adjustments to reduce battery consumption.

Finally, you can turn on battery saver mode in your device’s settings. This mode will allow your device to make more efficient use of its battery and reduce battery consumption significantly.

With these 5 easy steps, you can quickly check app battery usage on Android and optimise your device’s battery life. So, stop worrying about your device’s battery running out and start taking proactive steps to improve battery performance.

2. Uninstall and disable unused apps#

Uninstalling and disabling unused apps is one of the most important steps to reduce battery usage on any device, especially Android. Removing apps that are not used anymore will immediately free up system resources, allowing your device to run more efficiently and extend its battery life. Additionally, disabled apps won’t run in the background, helping to significantly reduce background app activities. To uninstall apps, locate it in the app drawer, tap on the app, then select “Uninstall” from the drop-down menu. To disable an app, go to the “Settings” app and select “Apps” from the main menu. Find the app you want to disable, tap it, then select “Disable”. These are two simple yet efficient ways to reduce battery usage on Android.
