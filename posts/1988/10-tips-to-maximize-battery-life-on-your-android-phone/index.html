  1. Limit Automatic Synchronization of Data: Syncing of data can put a lot of stress on your device’s battery. Try to sync data manually as much as possible and limit it to save battery power.

  2. Turn Off Background Apps: Background apps make your Android phone work extra hard and in turn drain your battery faster. Try to turn off background apps every once in a while or when not in use.

  3. Adjust Brightness: Adjusting the brightness of your Android phone will also help reduce battery consumption. Since the brighter the screen, the more power it needs.

  4. Reduce Screen Timeout: If you keep the screen active for longer periods of time, you will find that your battery will drain more quickly. Reducing the screen timeout to the lowest possible value while still being able to view content can help increase battery life.

  5. Switch Off GPS: GPS is a major battery drainer on Android phones, so if you’re not using it then switch it off when not in use to conserve battery power.

  6. Uninstall Unnecessary Apps: Unnecessary apps can also be a major culprit when it comes to draining your battery. Uninstalling apps that you don’t use can help conserve battery power.

  7. Use Power Saving Mode: Many Android phones come with a built-in power-saving mode. This mode disables certain features and helps to extend battery life.

  8. Use Airplane Mode: If you’re in a location with poor signal reception or simply want to conserve battery life, switch to airplane mode for a few hours. This will help reduce battery consumption.

  9. Use Dark Wallpapers: Using dark wallpapers as backgrounds helps to reduce battery usage. Since the darker colored backgrounds don’t need as much power as bright colored backgrounds.

  10. Keep Your Device Updated: Keeping your Android phone updated can also help to optimize battery performance. This is because the newer version of the OS can bring improved battery saving features.
